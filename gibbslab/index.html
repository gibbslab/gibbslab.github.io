GiBBS Laboratory

GiBBS computational laboratory is located at Institute for Genetics, National University of Colombia.

Our lab consists of various desktop machines, an intermediate capacity server (100GB RAM, 27 CPU cores) and a hyper convergent DELL VRTX cluster solution with the following overall characteristics:

  • Four power Edge M640 blade servers.
  • RAM: 2.8 TB
  • Storage: 120 TB
  • 280 CPU cores Xeon Gold 2.6 GhZ

Most Admin tasks are supported by Javier Duque. Email: jaduquec@unal.edu.co

Access to the platform

All students and researchers associated to the Institute for Genetics (IGUN) have granted access to the platform. In order to accomplish this, each principal investigator of the IGUN’s research groups can ask for the researcher account by email at ampinzonv@unal.edu.co.

The PI is therefore responsible for the activities of the student/co-worker on the platform and must agree with our policy access terms, describe below.

Access policy

Given that our human resources are still very limited, it is important to take into account the following considerations when accessing and using this bioinformatics platform:

BY ACCESSING GiBBS BIOINFORMATICS PLATFORM YOU AGREE WITH THE FOLLOWING TERMS:

  1. All data, analysis and associated results are responsibility of each user, GiBBS can not take any responsibility for any of your data, nor we make backups of the system. So please make sure of keeping your data safe and performing a backup of your information on the cloud or an external drive.

  2. Sadly, we are subjected to “sudden” electrical power cuts performed by the university. Although our servers are protected by two power plants, there is nothing we can do if during a running process, electricity is gone and you lose your work.

  3. We do our best to keep software up to date and to somehow advice researchers and help them to solve their computational issues, but since we do not have anyone dedicated to this particular job, most of the times all we can do is to give you some vague advice, so please do not expect a 24/7 like support.

  4. Access to the platform is granted only for academic and researcher porpoises, any suspicious activity that deviates from this should be avoided, and if detected reported to corresponding PI, since “precious” computational resources can be wasted and taken from other running jobs. It includes installing software that can harm other users data.

  5. For heavy computational processes such as metagenome/metatranscriptome or large genomes assemblies it is necessary to schedule a job before proceeding, this is necessary in order to make sure you will have enough computational resources to perform your task. If not, you are taking the risk of loosing your information.

We at GiBBS understand that by accessing this platform you agree with all the terms stated on this section.
